# How Scarcity Affects You and Your Clients

A training developed for MFIP and Tribal TANF frontline workers on how scarcity affects cognition and decision-making, and how social service systems can be better designed to account for these effects.

## Key Concepts

**Scarcity** — Having less of something (time, money, calories, bandwidth) than you feel you need.

**Bandwidth** — The brain's capacity to perform higher-order functions like planning, decision-making, and self-regulation. Scarcity taxes bandwidth.

**Tunneling** — When under scarcity, people focus narrowly on the most immediate need and neglect longer-term considerations.

**Hassle factors** — Small inconveniences that cause people to fail to act, even when they intend to.

**Poverty as chronic scarcity** — People with low incomes are not less capable; they face persistent bandwidth taxes that higher-income people do not.

---

## Three Principles for Designing for Scarcity

1. **Cut costs** — Reduce friction and inconvenience in systems and interactions (not budget cuts).
2. **Create slack** — Build in room for error; recognize that low-income individuals have no margin.
3. **Reframe and empower** — Use language and practices that treat families as experts in their own lives.
